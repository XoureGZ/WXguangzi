SoloSlate Privacy Policy

App: SoloSlate
Package name: com.soloslate.offline.kit
Last updated: June 12, 2026
Effective date: June 12, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how SoloSlate ("we", "our", or "the app") handles information when you use the SoloSlate Android application. SoloSlate is an offline toolkit for solo tabletop-style adventures.

Summary
  • No account, login, or cloud database is required.
  • We do not collect, sell, or share your personal data on our servers.
  • Data you enter is stored locally on your device.
  • No ads and no third-party analytics SDKs in version 1.0.
  • The app declares no sensitive Android runtime permissions.

1. Information We Collect

SoloSlate does not operate a backend service and does not collect personal information on our servers. We do not require you to create an account.

Information you choose to enter in the app is stored locally on your device, including:

  • Character records (names, stats, HP, notes, inventory items)
  • Campaign records and settings notes
  • Adventure log entries
  • Dice roll history and custom dice presets
  • Random tables and table roll history
  • Map grid notes
  • App preferences (theme, haptics, dice animation, history limit)
  • Onboarding completion status

This data remains on your device unless you export it, share it, or use Android system backup features described below.

2. How We Use Information

Locally stored information is used only to provide app functionality, such as displaying your characters, saving your campaigns, rolling dice, showing history, and restoring your preferences.

We do not use your information for advertising, profiling, or cross-app tracking.

3. Data Sharing

SoloSlate does not automatically upload your data to us or to third-party servers.

Data may leave your device only when you choose to:

  • Export a backup: You can export a JSON backup file through Android’s system file picker. You choose where the file is saved.
  • Import a backup: You can select a backup file through the system file picker to restore data.
  • Share an adventure log: You can share log text through Android’s system share sheet. The destination app or service is your choice.
  • Open the privacy policy link in a browser: If you tap the web version link in the app, your device’s browser handles the request. SoloSlate does not declare the INTERNET permission.

4. Permissions

SoloSlate’s Android manifest does not declare user-facing sensitive permissions such as location, camera, microphone, contacts, SMS, phone, calendar, Bluetooth, NFC, notifications, or storage read/write permissions.

Feature Permission declared? How it works
Core offline tools No Local Room database and DataStore on device
Backup export / import No Android Storage Access Framework; user selects a file
Share adventure log No Android system share intent
Haptic feedback No Standard view haptic feedback API
Timer / turn tracker No Runs in the app while open; no background alarm permission

Android libraries used by the app may include internal framework permissions that are not shown to users as install-time dangerous permissions. These are used only for normal Android app operation.

5. Third-Party Services

Version 1.0 of SoloSlate does not include third-party advertising SDKs, analytics SDKs, crash reporting SDKs, or social login SDKs.

If you choose to open an external link or share content to another app, that external service is governed by its own privacy policy.

6. Android Backup and Device Transfer

The app enables Android backup settings for local app data such as preferences and database content. If you use Google’s device backup or device-to-device transfer features on your Android device, your operating system may include SoloSlate data according to your device settings. This is controlled by Android and your account settings, not by SoloSlate servers.

7. Data Retention and Deletion

Your data remains on your device until you delete it or uninstall the app.

You can delete all local app data at any time from More > Data Management inside the app. Uninstalling SoloSlate removes local app data from your device, subject to your Android system behavior and any backup copies you may have stored elsewhere.

8. Children’s Privacy

SoloSlate is a general utility app and does not knowingly collect personal information from children through our own servers. Because the app stores user-entered content locally on the device, parents and guardians should supervise use as appropriate.

9. Security

We design SoloSlate as an offline-first app with local storage. However, no method of electronic storage is completely secure. You are responsible for securing your device, backup files, and any apps or services you use to store or share exported data.

10. International Users

SoloSlate is provided as a local device application. Because we do not operate a cloud backend for core app features, we do not intentionally transfer your app content to our servers.

11. Google Play Data Safety

For Google Play’s Data safety form, SoloSlate should be described as an app that does not collect or transmit user data to the developer’s servers. User-created content is stored locally on the device. Optional backup export is user-initiated and user-controlled.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when the app changes. If we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date on this page. Continued use of the app after an update means you accept the revised policy.
